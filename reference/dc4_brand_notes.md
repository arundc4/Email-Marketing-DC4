# Digital C4 (DC4) Brand Notes

Grounded in digitalc4.com (About Us page) and public sources, July 2026.

## Who we are
Digital C4 was founded in 2010 by Jason Nuss and Chuck Swanson to change how
agencies and clients work together. Based in Portland, Oregon.

- **Jason Q. Nuss** (Founder & Partner): 23+ years in B2B digital marketing,
  former COO at a managed services company. Focus: paid media strategy and
  client success.
- **Chuck Swanson** (Founder & Partner): 25+ years in direct and digital
  marketing, prior leadership at Intel, VMware, and FormFactor. Focus: SEO,
  AEO, and content strategy.

## Positioning
"B2B marketing strategy and execution. Built for pipeline."

DC4 positions itself as a partner, not a vendor: senior-level involvement,
accountability tied to pipeline and business outcomes rather than vanity
metrics (impressions, likes, etc).

**Tagline:** "Digital C4 delivers more than marketing. We deliver momentum."

## Who we serve
Mid-sized to enterprise B2B companies, primarily SaaS, technology,
education, and professional services. Buyers are typically marketing
directors, VPs, demand gen managers, and CMOs who need expert execution
without adding internal headcount.

## Services
- Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)
- Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
- Paid Media (Google Ads, LinkedIn, Meta)
- Content Strategy and Growth
- Conversion Rate Optimization and Landing Page Development
- Marketing Automation (HubSpot)
- White Label services for agencies

## How we work
Senior founders stay hands-on in client work. Engagements are built around
each client's specific goals rather than a generic playbook. Success is
measured by pipeline impact, not vanity metrics. Transparent, set pricing.

## Use in outreach
- Reference DC4 lightly, as an example or point of view, not a pitch, in
  early emails.
- Tie DC4's angle (senior-level partner focused on pipeline, not vanity
  metrics) to the specific challenge identified for the prospect's company.
- Keep any DC4 mention proportional to the email's place in the sequence
  (barely present in email 1, soft example in email 3, no pitch in email 2).
