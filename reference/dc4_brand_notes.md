# DC4 (Digital C4) Brand Notes

Source: "DC4 Brand Document" (Google Doc, Digital C4 Drive, 2026 Brand
Platform Draft) and "Jason DC4 Brand Voice" (Google Doc). Condensed here
for use in outreach drafting. Pulled in full on 2026-07-21. See the
source docs for the complete 15-section brand platform.

## Who they are
- Digital C4: senior-led B2B digital marketing agency, founded 2010
  (15+ years), HQ Portland, Oregon, with team in Boise and Boston.
- Founder & Partner: Jason Nuss. Both founders stay actively involved in
  client strategy and delivery, not just at kickoff.
- Operating model: extension of the client's internal team, not a
  detached vendor. Hybrid: senior strategy + hands-on execution.

## What they sell
- SEO (technical, on-page, content strategy, keyword research)
- AEO / Answer Engine Optimization (structuring content/technical/
  authority signals so AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI
  Overviews extract, trust, and cite the brand)
- Paid media: Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Meta, YouTube, display, remarketing
- Strategic content programs
- HubSpot / marketing automation, CRO, landing pages
- White-label services for other agencies

## Who they sell to
- B2B marketing decision-makers: CMO, VP Marketing, Marketing Director,
  Demand Gen Leader, Growth Marketing Manager, Founder/Exec Sponsor.
- ICP: mid-size to enterprise B2B with a considered buying process,
  existing marketing team/leader, budget for agency support. Especially
  strong fit: SaaS, technology, cybersecurity, education, life sciences,
  professional services.

## Core positioning and messaging (use verbatim where natural)
- "Activity Doesn't Build Pipeline. We help B2B marketing teams build the
  programs that do."
- "Clicks are easy to buy. Pipeline requires strategy."
- "Ranking in search is one part of the equation. Appearing in
  AI-generated answers is the other." / "Visibility is no longer about
  being found. It's about being selected."
- Brand promise: "Digital C4 turns digital marketing activity into
  measurable B2B momentum."
- Short positioning line: "B2B marketing strategy and execution. Built
  for pipeline."
- Master line: "Digital C4: B2B Marketing Built for Pipeline."

## Audience pain points -> DC4 response (use to pick the challenge angle)
- "We're doing a lot, but not seeing enough pipeline" -> DC4 connects
  activity to revenue-oriented programs.
- "Our internal team is stretched" -> DC4 works as an extension of the
  team.
- "Paid spend is rising, quality is inconsistent" -> optimize for intent,
  conversion, pipeline quality, not just clicks.
- "Our content gets traffic but doesn't convert" -> structured content
  programs aligned with authority and revenue.
- "We're not ready for AI search" -> AEO/GEO content and authority
  structuring.
- "Our reports don't tell us what to do next" -> reporting as strategy,
  not a performance summary.

## Voice principles that should shape outreach specifically
- Lead with the business problem, not the channel/service.
- Keep the client as the hero, DC4 as the guide. Never position DC4 as
  the hero.
- Translate data/observations into meaning, not just description.
- No generic agency claims ("full-service digital marketing solutions")
  without context; no channel-first messaging.

## Proof points (only use if genuinely relevant and true, never invent)
- Public clients referenced: NWEA, Babel Street, Apromore, Mercalis,
  Intellimize.
- NWEA VP quote: partnership "systematically helped us build a
  significant audience" over five years.

## How this should shape the 4-email sequence
- Email 1 should read like a peer noticing something real and specific
  about the recipient's site/company/market, then naming the single most
  relevant pain point from the list above, not a generic pitch.
- Keep DC4 mentions soft and example-driven until email 3; email 3 can
  reference DC4's approach (SEO/AEO/paid/content/HubSpot as fits their
  actual situation) as a light example, not a sales pitch.
- Never invent case studies, client names, or stats that aren't sourced
  above; if a stat would help, use `[STAT NEEDED]` per the voice guide
  instead of fabricating one.
